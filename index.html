about

I'm a person who's always driven by new ideas and new challenges, especially in the areas of communities and collaboration. I am fascinated with technology and its advancements, especially in the online and mobile arenas, striving each day to keep up with new development and innovation.

I'm also a huge fan of the opensource and hacker culture, which were my main drivers in founding linuxNUS and HackerspaceSG. It is my personal belief that increased adoption of the hackers' exploratory culture will be immensely beneficial to the rate of innovation and creativity here in Singapore. As such, even though I've graduated from NUS, I still play an active role in the linuxNUS Coreteam in trying to cultivate more hackers in my alma mater.

I'm a hacker at heart, and nothing gets me more excited than a new project in which technology can be applied to make life easier, or more fun, for those involved. It's not just the technology itself that keeps me pumped, but it's using technology in a way that will bring value to someone else's life. Knowing that you've made someone happy through your skills - there's no greater reward.

NUS Overseas Colleges Stockholm Jul 2006 - Jul 2007

The NUS Overseas Colleges (NOC) programme sends students to a number of innovation-rich cities around the world, and has them working in start-up companies in order to experience first hand the culture of entrepreneurship. NOC locations currently include the Silicon Valley in California, Bio Valley in Philadelphia, Shanghai and Beijing in China, Stockholm in Sweden, Bangalore in India and Tel Aviv in Israel.

I spent a year in Stockholm working with Mobile Sorcery (now renamed MoSync) as a software engineer. MoSync is a cross-platform mobile development toolkit that allows application authors to deploy applications written in MoSync to multiple mobile platforms with just a single codebase.

Founder, linuxNUS - the NUS Students' Linux User Group 2005

Finding a lack of support for Linux and Open Source Software in NUS, I founded linuxNUS in order to build a community for those with similar interests.

From a simple posting in the then-active NUS School of Computing BBS, linuxNUS kicked off with a 10-member strong core team and is still in operation in 2010, 5 years down the road. Along the way, we have campaigned for increased support for Linux and Open Source in NUS, held events, workshops and hackfests meant to bring FOSS to the students and have provided support for the community where we can.

For the sake of outreach and community building, we run multiple social channels, ranging from a mailing list, a blog, a wiki, an IRC channel, and a Twitter account among others.
